Frequently Asked Questions

FoodLens is an AI-powered tool that analyzes images of fruits & vegetables to estimate their freshness or rottenness. By using computer vision, it helps users identify produce quality before purchase or consumption.

When you upload an image, our trained AI model analyzes color, texture, and visual features unique to each type of produce. It then generates a freshness score based on patterns it learned from thousands of labeled images.

Currently, FoodLens can analyze:

  • Apples
  • Bananas
  • Bell Peppers
  • Bitter Gourds
  • Cucumbers
  • Okras
  • Oranges
  • Potatoes
  • Tomatoes

We plan to expand to more items in future versions.

Our model achieves an average accuracy of 85–90%, depending on lighting conditions and image clarity. Continuous training and dataset updates help improve accuracy over time.

For best results:

  • Use natural lighting (avoid harsh shadows).
  • Place the produce on a plain background.
  • Ensure the entire fruit or vegetable is visible in the frame.

FoodLens was created by a team of five senior students at the University of Louisiana at Lafayette. Each team member contributed a different area of expertise including AI development, data integration, UI/UX design, frontend development, and database management to build a user-friendly, AI-powered tool that helps people assess the freshness of their produce.
